## HOMEPAGE COPY — SECTION REWRITES

---

### HERO / OPENING STATEMENT

**CURRENT FRAMING (from notes):**
Language in this section references people "falling through the cracks" and positions TSE around what it "closes."

**THE PROBLEM:**
"Falling through the cracks" centers the failure. It also implies people are passive. TSE's work is about what happens *next* — the path that exists when someone shows up to do the coordination work. That's the story worth telling.

"What TSE Closes" frames the work as reactive and puts the word "closes" in the headline — which is vague, jargon-adjacent, and off-brand.

---

**OPTION A — Lead with the work:**

> Some people have support lined up and still don't land anywhere.
>
> Not because the programs aren't there. Because nobody was holding the thread between them.
>
> That's the work TSE does.

---

**OPTION B — Lead with what changes:**

> Referrals are just the beginning.
>
> What happens between the referral and the resolution — that's where people either move forward or wait indefinitely. TSE works in that space. On purpose.

---

**OPTION C — Most direct:**

> Care coordination doesn't fail because providers don't care.
> It pauses because no one has the bandwidth to hold every piece together.
>
> TSE holds those pieces.

---

*Recommendation: Option A or C. Option A is the most narrative and has momentum. Option C is punchy and direct — best if the visual design already tells part of the story.*

This section needs a new name and new framing. Options below:

**Section Name — Options:**
- What TSE Makes Possible
- Where TSE Steps In
- What Changes When TSE Is in the Room
- What Forward Movement Looks Like

*Recommendation: "What TSE Makes Possible" — it's active, it's forward-facing, and it positions TSE as an enabler, not a fixer of broken things.*

### "FALLING THROUGH THE CRACKS" — REPLACEMENT LANGUAGE

Wherever this phrase appears, use one of the following instead:

| Banned phrase | Replacement options |
|---|---|
| Falling through the cracks | lost between programs / waiting without movement / left without a next step |
| Gap in services | pause in the pathway / breakdown between referral and follow-through |
| Closes the gap | bridges referral to resolution / moves a case forward / gets to resolution |
| What TSE closes | what TSE makes possible / where TSE works / what changes with TSE in the room |
| Stuck / stalled | waiting / paused / without a clear next step |

## LANGUAGE REFERENCE — USE THROUGHOUT

These are the replacements that apply everywhere on the site, not just the homepage.

**Never use:**
- gap / gaps
- closes / TSE closes
- falling through the cracks
- stuck or stalled
- silos
- folks
- throughput / bottleneck
- move (applied to people)
- infrastructure (minimize — only use with something concrete)

**Reach for instead:**
- pauses / pause in the pathway
- forward movement
- referral to resolution
- supports / programs / pathways
- navigates / coordinates / holds the thread
- gets to resolution
